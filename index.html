Building Your Digital Home

A website is more than just pictures; it's a digital handshake. Let's explore the essential rooms (sections) of a photography website, styled with your unique "Little Image" warmth.

Click through each section to see exactly how every element works, then explore interactive gallery layouts you can open, click, and experience.

1. The Hero Section

The Mechanics

This is the "SearchLight". It needs to answer three questions immediately:

  • Who are you?
  • What do you do?
  • Where do you do it?

Why it matters

Users decide in 0.05 seconds if they like your site. A strong image + clear text hooks them instantly.

1 Full-Width Background Image

Your strongest portfolio photo fills the entire hero. Uses background-size: cover so it always fills the space beautifully, regardless of screen size. Pick an image that tells the story of your brand instantly.

2 Dark Overlay Layer

A semi-transparent black overlay (rgba(0,0,0,0.3)) sits on top of the image. This ensures white text is always readable, even over bright photos. It's invisible to the user but critical for accessibility.

3 Business Name / Heading

Large, serif font (Solway) centered over the image. This is the first thing visitors see. It should be your studio name - clean, confident, and positioned with plenty of breathing room.

4 Tagline / Subheading

One line that says exactly what you do and who you serve. "Capturing snuggly moments for modern mothers" tells location-less value. Keep it under 10 words.

5 Call-to-Action Button

The single most important clickable element. Pill-shaped, sage green, with a hover animation that lifts the button. Guides visitors directly to the gallery or booking page. Never have more than one CTA in the hero.

2. The Connection (About Me)

What is this section?

This is where "Business" meets "Person". Clients are inviting you into their personal space - they need to know who you are before they book you.

Key Elements:

  • A Friendly Portrait - approachable, not behind a camera.
  • Your "Why" - why you take photos.
  • Personal Tidbits - relatable details.

1 The Friendly Portrait

Not a headshot behind the camera - a warm, approachable photo of YOU. Uses rounded corners and a subtle floating animation (translateY over 6 seconds) that gives the image a dreamy, gentle feel.

2 Split Layout (50/50 Grid)

CSS Grid with grid-template-columns: 1fr 1fr. Image on the left, text on the right. This mirrors how people naturally read - image catches the eye, text fills in the story. On mobile it stacks vertically.

3 First-Name Greeting

"Hi, I'm Rachel" - not "About the Photographer." First name creates instant familiarity. Paired with your "why" statement and style description to show personality before skill.

4 Signature Quote

An italic quote in the accent color (Muted Rose) that sums up your philosophy. It's styled differently from body text to stand out as the emotional anchor of the section.

3. The Proof (Gallery Strategy)

Order out of Chaos

Instead of dumping 500 images into one page, we create "Buckets of Interest".

The User Flow:

  1. Arrives: "I need baby photos."
  2. Sees categories: Newborn, Milestones, Occasions.
  3. Clicks one: Full gallery opens.

This prevents overwhelm and helps them find what they want faster.

4. The Love Letters (Reviews)

The Mechanics

Social proof. Don't call them "Reviews" - call them "Love Notes" or "Kind Words".

Key Elements:

  • Heart Icon - softer than quotation marks.
  • Real Quotes - emotional language works best.
  • Family Name - personal, not corporate.

Seeing others rave validates the investment.

5. The Answers (FAQ)

The Mechanics

Address fears upfront. "What do I wear?" "What if my baby cries?"

Key Elements:

  • Accordion - click to reveal answers.
  • Chevron - rotates to show state.
  • Hover Color - signals interactivity.

Answering before they ask shows you are the expert guide.

Common Questions

What should we wear?
Soft, neutral tones work best - think creams, tans, and muted greens. Avoid bold logos or busy patterns. I'll send you a full style guide once you book!
What if my baby won't stop crying?
Babies cry - it's totally normal! I build extra time into every session so we can pause for feedings, diaper changes, and cuddles. Patience is my superpower.
Can I bring my dog?
Absolutely! Fur babies are family too. I love incorporating pets into family sessions - some of my favorite shots include the family dog.
How long until I get my photos?
You'll receive a sneak peek within 48 hours and your full gallery within 2-3 weeks. Each image is carefully edited to match the Little Image style.
