Design Presentation

Building Your Digital Home

A website is more than just pictures; it's a digital handshake. Let's explore the essential rooms (sections) of a photography website, styled with your unique "Little Image" warmth.

1. The Hero Section

The Mechanics

This is the "SearchLight". It needs to answer three questions immediately:

  • Who are you?
  • What do you do?
  • Where do you do it?

Why it matters

Users decide in 0.05 seconds if they like your site. A strong image + clear text hooks them instantly.

Little Image Photography

Capturing snuggly moments for modern mothers.

2. The Connection (About Me)

What is this section?

This is where "Business" meets "Person". It's not just a biography; it's an opportunity to build trust. For a photographer, clients are inviting you into their personal space (their weddings, their homes, their newborn's nursery). They need to know who you are before they book you.

Key Elements We Include:

  • A Friendly Portrait: A photo of YOU looking approachable (not hiding behind a camera).
  • Your "Why": Why do you take photos? (e.g., "I love preserving memories").
  • Personal Tidbits: Small details (like coffee lover, dog dad) that make you relatable.

3. The Proof (Gallery Strategy)

The Strategy: Order out of Chaos

Instead of dumping 500 images into one page, we guide the user. We create "Buckets of Interest".

The User Flow:

  1. User arrives: "I need photos for my baby."
  2. They see categories: Newborn, Milestones, Occasions.
  3. They click "Newborn": Now they see the full gallery.

This prevents overwhelm and helps them find exactly what they are looking for faster.

4. The Love Letters (Reviews)

The Mechanics

Social proof. Don't call them "Reviews" - call them "Love Notes" or "Kind Words".

Why it matters

It builds trust. Seeing others rave about their "snuggly moments" validates the investment.

"I usually hate being in photos, but this felt like hanging out with a friend. The results made me cry happy tears."

- The Miller Family

5. The Answers (FAQ)

The Mechanics

Address fears upfront. "What do I wear?" "What if my baby cries?" "How much is it?"

Why it matters

It reduces friction. Answering "What do I wear?" before they ask shows you are the expert guide.

Advanced Features

The Gallery Lab

Exploring dynamic ways to showcase the "Little Image" portfolio.

1. The Organic Masonry

Strict grids feel corporate. Masonry layouts feel like a collage on a wall. Perfect for mixing vertical and horizontal shots.

2. The Cinematic Film Strip

A horizontal scroll that mimics looking through negatives. Great for telling a linear story of a wedding day.

6. The Invitation (Contact)

The Mechanics

The finish line. Keep it simple. Name, date, and a warm "Let's Chat" button.

Why it matters

Don't make them hunt for it. This is the goal of the entire website.
